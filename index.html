I don't sugarcoat. I tell you what happened, what I experienced — even when it's uncomfortable, even when it hurt, even when it was my own mistake.

I've spent 18+ years managing software and hardware development projects in the automotive industry — safety-critical work, ASIL D level, the kind where the consequences of failure are real.

At some point I looked around and realised something: in the impersonal world of a large multi, you don't matter. No manager genuinely cares about your results, the wins, or the battles you've been through. The moment you stop being commercially useful — you're out. And all that experience walks out with you.

So I asked myself: what if I built something that can't be taken away?

That's what this is. Two decades of PM knowledge — escalations, stakeholder dynamics, silent failures, scope negotiations, executive communication — packaged into tools that actually work. Shared peer-to-peer. From me to you. Avoid being eaten up by the multis.
